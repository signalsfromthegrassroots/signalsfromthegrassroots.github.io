Apply now to be the next global food systems leader through the United Nations(Funding available for all nationalities)

Are you ready to be a driving force in reshaping the future of food systems worldwide? Seize the opportunity to participate in an inspiring program designed for early career professionals like you. Supported by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ), this initiative aims to accelerate solutions for food systems in the Global South.

Program Highlights:

Selected participants will engage in a 5-day, in-person leadership workshop, fostering connections among innovators, practitioners, scientists, and activists from low- and middle-income countries. Together, the focus is on connecting policy advocacy with grassroots action to collectively shape a positive future for food.

Learn, Get Inspired, Act:

  1. Engage in a 5-day co-creative workshop, bridging the gap between data, knowledge, and implementation.
  2. Explore modules such as negotiations, policy advocacy, strategic communication, and evidence-based decision-making.
  3. Immerse yourself in presentations by seasoned practitioners, scientists, and young global food champions.

What the Program Covers:

  1. Governance Systems: Negotiations and design of multi-stakeholder processes for food systems governance.
  2. Policy Advocacy: Master leadership skills for policy advocacy, bridging the gap from global to local levels.
  3. Communication and Storytelling: Craft impactful communication and storytelling strategies.
  4. Science and Evidence-based Decision-making: Build proposals and partnerships based on scientific evidence.

Why Join?

This dynamic program aligns with the UN Food Systems Summit, addressing the shortage of skilled personnel and youth advocacy needs. Be part of a movement that breaks institutional silos, driving real change in food systems.

Expected Outcomes:

  1. Enhance your ability to drive systems change in partnership with the UN Food Systems Summit National Convenors.
  2. Develop plans or initiatives for food systems transformation, with potential grants in the second phase to scale up impactful programs.

Who Are We Looking For?

Early career professionals from all disciplines engaged in food systems activities. Proficiency in English is desirable. Apply now and transform your vision into action.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Age between 18-35 years old.
  2. Nationals from low and middle-income countries.
  3. Proven track record in food systems transformation innovation, action, and advocacy.

Application Timeline:

  1. Until January 15, 2024: Submit your application.
  2. End of January: Participants selected for a scholarship are informed.
  3. First week of February: Participants respond to the invitation.
  4. End of February: Participants receive program information.

Selection Criteria:

Candidates will be assessed based on current work, academic/professional excellence, policy advocacy/communication skills, leadership experience, motivation, and more.

Time Commitment:

Selected participants commit to an entire week for the in-person seminar and travel. Post-workshop, participants are encouraged to initiate and coordinate activities aligned with the learning outcomes.

Workshop Details

Scheduled for March to April 2024, specific dates and locations will be confirmed soon.

Funding Opportunities

Sponsorship covers travel, accommodations, and meals for selected candidates. Self-funded participants are responsible for their expenses. No workshop participation fee.

Online Access

While not live-streamed, a toolkit with key modules and discussions will be available in the second phase for a broader audience.

Post-Workshop Impact

The program offers a unique opportunity to leverage leadership skills, expand networks, and contribute to the transformation of food systems. Successful candidates are expected to continue their initiatives in partnership with their governments.
