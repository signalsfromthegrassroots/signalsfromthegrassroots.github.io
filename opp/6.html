The York Global Widening Participation Scholarship 2023, UK: Apply Now!

Overview

The York University international Pre-university Summer School is open to all students aged 16-18 in secondary or further education, who are planning to enter higher education and are ready to get a taste of what it means to study at a top university in the UK. This two-week summer school is a combination of serious academic study and fun-packed social activities – all in an international community, based on York University's parkland campus, delivered by the University’s academic departments.

Three full scholarships are available for secondary/high school students from Latin America, Sub-Saharan Africa and Ukraine.

What is Value of Award?

The York Global Widening Participation Scholarship covers the following:

  1. programme fee (£3,497)
  2. travel costs (according to the University’s travel and expenses policy)
  3. passport and visa costs (up to £300)
  4. £100 spending money

Further benefit: University Placement

If you successfully complete the programme, York University guarantees that you will be invited to interview or receive a typical offer for a place to study on an undergraduate programme at the University of York.
This offer is only available to international students (including EU). To be eligible you will need to:

  1. have successfully completed the Pre-university Summer School programme of York Global Summer Schools
  2. have the potential to meet the typical entry requirements (both academic and English language) for your chosen programme
  3. complete an online application via UCAS (the Universities and Colleges - Admissions Service) by the January equal consideration deadline within two years of completing York Global Summer Schools.

Eligibility Criteria

  1. you are currently in secondary education
  2. you hold a citizenship from any country in Latin America or Sub-Saharan Africa or from Ukraine
  3. you meet our minimum recommended level of English language skills, ie CEFR B2, IELTS 5, TOEFL 44+ or Duolingo 75+ (no need for a language exam)
  4. you can prove your academic motivation
  5. your family’s financial situation would not enable you to participate in the programme without this scholarship
  6. this would be your first study abroad experience.

You are more likely to get the scholarship if:

  1. neither of your parents attended university (or they went to university as a mature student during your secondary education)
  2. you are estranged from your family
  3. you are from a military family (while you have been in education, your parent(s) have served in the military)
  4. you have a disability or long term health condition*
  5. you belong to an ethnic minority group in your country.

*Due to the study abroad nature of the programme, you must be able to travel independently.

Documents Required for Application

Personal statement:

As part of your application, we ask you to tell us about your academic motivation to join the programme. We are interested in reading about the following:

  1. your general interest in higher education
  2. your interest in studying in the UK and at the University of York
  3. your specific interest in the academic option (subject) that you chose
  5. your interest in the other components of the York Global Summer Schools programme
  6. examples of your previous engagement with the subject that you chose

You can write up to 4,000 characters about it on the application form. We recommend that you write this in a word processor first and then copy it to the application form.

School support letter:

As part of your application, we also ask you to provide an official letter from your school supporting your application.You have to ask an employee of your current school (eg a teacher, counsellor or principal) to complete the form below. They can fill it in using a computer, but they must sign and stamp it, before giving you a scanned copy to submit.You will then need to upload this scanned copy of the school support letter as part of your scholarship application form.
