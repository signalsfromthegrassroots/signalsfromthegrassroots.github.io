The Deoitte Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4 ($50,000 grant)

Introduction

In the pursuit of a sustainable and equitable future, education stands as a fundamental pillar that has the power to uplift individuals, nations, and societies. Recognizing the transformative potential of education, Deloitte, a global leader in professional services, actively supports the Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4, now in its fifth year. This commitment aligns with Deloitte’s broader vision of empowering 100 million individuals by 2030 through enhanced job skills, improved educational outcomes, and opportunities for underrepresented and marginalized groups globally.

Deloitte’s SDG 4 Vision

Deloitte proudly sponsors the Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4, reinforcing their dedication to accelerating innovative youth-led solutions that promote inclusive, equitable, and quality education for all.

The Challenge's Criteria

In line with the overarching goal of preparing 100 million futures for a world of opportunity by 2030, the Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4 seeks to support scalable youth-led solutions that:

Candidates should;

  1. Utilize Innovative Approaches and Technology: Encourage the use of cutting-edge solutions to address educational challenges.
  2. Address Root Causes: Tackle the underlying issues contributing to educational inequality.
  3. Drive Impact and Potential to Scale: Demonstrate tangible results and the potential for broad-scale implementation.
  4. Collaboration and Recognition: Recognize existing efforts and collaborate with other stakeholders to amplify impact.
  5. Facilitate Collective Impact: Engage a diverse range of stakeholders to create opportunities for collective impact.
  6. Improve Quality and Access: Enhance the quality of, or access to, education and skills training for youth empowerment.
The challenge invites participation from young individuals affiliated with social enterprises, non-profits, or community organizations, with projects based in locations where Deloitte has a presence.

Areas of Impact

The Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4 encourages solutions that drive impact in at least one of the following areas:
  1. Skills Development and Lifelong Learning: Create opportunities for young people to acquire skills for the evolving workforce.
  2. Entrepreneurial Skills Acceleration: Cultivate entrepreneurial skills to foster a new generation of job creators.
  3. Inclusive Access to Education: Improve access to education and skills for those at risk of being left behind by the rapidly changing global economy.
  4. Digital Skills Empowerment: Support digital skills and the adoption of technologies to empower learners’ access to education and skill development opportunities.

Prize

The winning solution will receive:
  1. Sponsorship to participate in the One Young World Summit 2024
  2. A US$50,000 grant from Deloitte
  3. 12 months of mentorship from a team of Deloitte professionals and partners. The mentorship team will work to accelerate your solution based on the needs of your initiative or organisation, such as business strategy, best practices for data collection, monitoring and evaluation, and product design.

Application Details

Applications for the Lead2030 Challenge for SDG 4 close on March 1, 2024. Young innovators and change-makers are encouraged to apply now, contributing to the collective effort to shape a future where education is a catalyst for positive transformation.

Timeline

  1. 1 March 2024: Applications close
  2. 1 April 2024: Shortlisted candidates confirmed and invited to interview
  3. Mid-April 2024: Candidate interviews
  4. 15 April 2024: Challenge winners confirmed
