Hansen Leadership Institute Fellowship Program 2024 in USA (Fully Funded and open to US and non-USA citizens)

Overview

The Hansen Summer Institute 2024 is geared towards enhancing interactive and communication skills by facilitating encounters with individuals from diverse backgrounds and mindsets. Proficiency in English is essential to comprehend issues and actively participate in discussions. As part of the application process, two letters of reference are required to verify language proficiency—one from an academic professor or advisor and another from an NGO or community organization where you have contributed.

Academic Background

This opportunity is particularly relevant for individuals pursuing or with degrees in international relations, international economic development, diplomacy, or related fields. Don’t miss the chance to experience the USA on a fully funded scholarship and further your understanding of global leadership.

Program Details

  1. Duration: July 01, 2024, to July 23, 2024
  2. Host Country: United States of America
  3. Funded By: Fred J. Hansen Foundation

The Vision

The Hansen Leadership Institute is a professional development program initiated by the visionary Fred J. Hansen. The program focuses on leadership development, mediation, conflict resolution, and international cooperation. Fred J. Hansen’s vision is to create a peaceful world through international cooperation by bringing individuals from different nations to the United States. This experience allows participants to learn about American culture, traditions, economy, and government, fostering understanding and mutual respect.

Program Benefits

  1. International airfare is provided.
  2. Accommodation: Rooms and boarding facilities are provided.
  3. Costs: All program costs are covered.

Eligibility Criteria

For Non-U.S. Students:
  1. Same eligibility criteria as U.S. students.
  2. Recommendation from an academic advisor for English proficiency verification.
  3. Possession of a valid international passport.
  4. U.S. Visa is required.
  5. Preference for students who have not visited the U.S. before.
For U.S. Students:
  1. Upper-division undergraduates.
  2. Specialization in international relations, international economic development, diplomacy, or related fields.
  3. Age between 20 to 25 at the program’s commencement.
  4. Demonstrated leadership skills in community, nation, or region.
  5. Relevant experience working with NGOs or the community.

Application Deadline

  1. For International applicants: January 15, 2024.
  2. For U.S. applicants: March 15, 2024.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the official website.
  2. Click on the “Apply” button.
  3. Create an account and fill in the required information.
  4. Upload the necessary documentation.
  5. Carefully complete and submit your application form.
