FUNDED: Apply for the ACE Hub Youth Event!

Overview

The Action for Climate Empowerment Hub (ACE Hub) is a three-year collaboration between the Ministry of Economic Affairs, Industry, Climate Action and Energy of the State of North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW), Germany and UN Climate Change aimed at fostering the implementation of Article 12 of the ParisAgreement related to education and public awareness, training, public participation, and public access to information known as Action for Climate Empowerment (ACE), in NRW and globally.

Dates & Place

The ACE Hub Youth Event will be organized in-person from 5 - 7 June 2024 (TBC, please note the dates are tentative and subject to change) in Bonn, Germany, in English and in collaboration with multiple partners. Participants will include 25 international youth and 25 youth from NRW.

Focus

This year’s event will focus on community-based climate advocacy, action and initiatives at the local and subnational level. The event will take place over a period of 3 days, comprised of modules centered around skills development. Some aspects of a hackathon methodology such as interactive training sessions led by experts which foster leadership and encourage group collaboration will be incorporated into the modules. Participants may also have the opportunity to observe local climate action at the city level through an off-campus excursion.

Benefits

  1. Participants will have the opportunity to attend the 60th session of the subsidiary bodies (SBs) at the World Conference Center Bonn, including the Expert Dialogue on Children and Climate Change and the ACE Gallery on 4 June (tbc).
  2. Participants will also have the opportunity to exchange and network with fellow youth participants to share knowledge, skills and learn from each other. Opportunities to engage with regional and international experts will also be possible.

ELIGIBILITY

Applicants must meet the following criteria:
  1. Be between the ages of 18 – 35 at the time of the event;
  2. Be fluent in English (orally);
  3. Be able to participate in person for the full duration of the event;
  4. Have an interest in or experience with one or more elements of ACE, i.e., climate education and public awareness, training, public participation and access to information; and
  5. Ideally involved with or working on climate change topics at the local level, with cities, municipalities or subnational governments on community-based climate advocacy, action and initiatives.
*For local (NRW) applicants: Currently reside in the region of North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany.
